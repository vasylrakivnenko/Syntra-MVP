The Challenge: Syntra (General Counsel for SMBs)
SMBs and mid-market companies are under-served by legal support: outside counsel is expensive and slow, and in-house counsel usually arrives too late. The wedge is an AI-native general counsel product that ingests contracts or legal requests, compares them to company positions, drafts redlines and responses, flags risk, and routes high-risk items to a supervising attorney.

Show grounded contract review where every redline and risk flag cites company agreements, policies, or surfaced clauses from the counterparty document.

Ingest an inbound contract or legal request, produce a redline plus plain-language risk summary grounded in company positions and policies, and route flagged items to a supervising attorney with full context attached.

Every redline and risk flag must cite company knowledge or the source document.
Attorney-in-the-loop enforcement for issues above a risk threshold.
Privilege-aware access controls and a full audit trail of model actions.
At least one depth area: contract review, company playbooks, attorney queue design, privilege controls, audit trail and version history, or a procurement/employment specialization.
HOW WE EVALUATE
Working Prototype is the main event.
Build evidence beats polish: source code remains required build evidence, and demo video is not a substitute for source code or a runnable prototype.
Rough is fine. Candidates can use any tools and AI assistants they want.
A polished prompt, generated document, or thin wrapper around an LLM is not enough and will not be panel-ready.
Reviewers look at demo video, TDD, and PRD first, then verify the implementation through the prototype and source code.
PRD and TDD should each be 1-2 pages and act as show-your-work documents.
PRD
Define the operator user and supervising attorney, explain why an AI-native GC fits the SMB gap, and map the expansion from contract review to broader legal operations.

TDD
Cover DOCX/PDF/email ingestion, retrieval over company knowledge, clause extraction and comparison, explainable redline generation, attorney review queue design, and the audit trail schema.
